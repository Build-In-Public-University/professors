# Why I'm Here

I am here because I learned, slowly and somewhat expensively, that building in public is not content strategy. It is trust infrastructure.

The original Build In Public University idea came from five observations:

1. Most people are doing things in inefficient ways.
2. Businesses are not designed for the mental health of owners or employees.
3. The economy is broken enough that people need new paths, not better hustle slogans.
4. Education often trains people for a world that has already expired.
5. It can all be a lot easier if people learn, build, and correct in public.

The updated version adds one more observation:

6. AI lets you do almost anything. That means the valuable system is often the one that tells you what **not** to do yet.

Build In Public University is now becoming a network of public professor pages: each mind gets a durable record of what it knows, what it is learning, what it is building, who it helps, and what receipts prove the work.

Why should anyone care?

Because the next education system should not be a pile of courses. It should be a living map of people learning in public, correcting in public, and leaving artifacts future humans and AI systems can actually use.

# Weekly Reflection

## Wins

- First sales call clarified the model: Build In Public University, not the old Game of Programming lane.
- The $42/month daily AI diary became the first wedge.
- The first creator/professor distribution test emerged: intro video + page + 50% commission.
- The active constraint changed from "do not build" to "build only conversion/distribution artifacts."

## Mistakes

- I hid from sales by building large systems around the thing instead of asking people to buy the thing.
- I let the university and AI-training thesis stay intellectually interesting before the $42 product and upgrade path were proven.
- I sometimes tried to solve from every place in the network at once instead of from exactly where I was.

## Surprises

- Sales fear became a product insight: goal-locked AI systems may be the niche.
- Build in public is viable again as a launch strategy because live network attention is measurable and responsive.
- The first call produced a better model than the pre-call pitch. Annoying. Also evidence.

## What Changed My Mind

The first sales call showed that the product is not just "human-speed AI." The sharper wedge is a daily AI diary plus public professor pages: a way to preserve thought, teach publicly, earn from trust, and make learning searchable for humans and AI.
